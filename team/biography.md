<div id='christopher_ing' class="content">

    <h2><strong>Christopher Ing</strong></h2>
    <div class="content">
      <img src="/team/photos/Christopher_Ing.jpg">
      <img src="/team/photos/mol_chris.png">
    </div>
    <p>
      I aim to understand the biophysical mechanisms of wild-type and mutant cytochrome c oxidase using all-atom molecular dynamics. I am particularly interested in ion movement and solvation in these proteins.
      <br>
      Under the guidance of <b><a href="http://pnroy.uwaterloo.ca/">Dr. Pierre Nicholas Roy</a></b>, I calculated properties of small quantum clusters systems at ultra low temperatures. To achieve this I utilized a path integral molecular dynamics sampling method which I assisted in implementing in the <b><a href="http://dirac.cnrs-orleans.fr/MMTK/">Molecular Modelling Toolkit</a></b>. The simulation of path integral helium about a fixed CO2 impurity is <b><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zziYdYFpOac">shown here</a></b> for a 64 time-slice helium atom.
      <br>
      I am also actively involved in studying the molecular structure and function of other membrane proteins like the voltage-gated sodium channel. I am currently working on Python software to assist in the analysis of timeseries data from multiple simulation repeats.
    </p>
  </div>
